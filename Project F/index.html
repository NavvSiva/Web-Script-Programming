About Me

I am currently a second-year student at the University of Ontario Tech, majoring in Networking and IT Security. My passion for technology and security has driven me to pursue a career in this wonderful field.

Before diving into the world of networking and security, I completed an advanced diploma in Aviation Operations. I’ve always been fascinated by systems that work behind the scenes, whether it’s planes in the sky or cybersecurity. The aviation world taught me precision, quick thinking, and how to keep cool when things get turbulent.

I believe that understanding both the technical and human aspects of security is essential, and I’m excited to keep learning and growing in this field, contributing to a safer and more connected world.

    Hardware Encryption

    In this project, I explored the role of hardware encryption in securing automotive systems, wireless sensor networks, instant messaging, and IoT devices. My research highlights the shift from traditional Trusted Platform Modules (TPMs) to firmware-based TPMs, embedded directly within CPUs. This integration enhances system security and efficiency. Key findings from IEEE papers emphasize how hardware encryption improves energy efficiency, operational speed, and resilience against cyber threats.

    CryptoMine Network Configuration

    As part of a group, I helped develop a comprehensive network solution for CryptoMine, a newly merged cryptocurrency mining company with branches across North America. Using Cisco Packet Tracer, our team configured a dedicated WAN using BGP to connect offices in Chicago, Vancouver, Toronto, and Mexico. The project included setting up VLANs, EtherChannel, OSPF routing, BGP, inter-VLAN routing, and trunking across multiple sites. We also designed a secure remote access solution from Ontario Tech Residence, allowing full management of the network infrastructure.

    EV Charging Station Route Optimization

    In this group project for my Algorithms and Data Structures class, we developed an EV Charging Station Route Optimization application using Python. By implementing Dijkstra’s algorithm, we calculated the shortest path to charging stations, helping EV users navigate efficiently. The project involved collecting data in a CSV format, creating a graph of nodes and connections, and utilizing Python libraries like NetworkX and Matplotlib for visualization. This project demonstrated how algorithms can optimize real-world navigation challenges, enhancing EV charging infrastructure.
